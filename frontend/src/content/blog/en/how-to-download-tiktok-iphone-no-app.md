## Why You Shouldn't Use the In-App "Save Video" Button

Using TikTok's native save feature has three main drawbacks:
1.  **Annoying Watermark:** It embeds a bouncing watermark with the creator's username that covers the edges of the video.
2.  **Reduced Quality:** TikTok often compresses the video file, resulting in a lower-quality version than the original.
3.  **It's Often Disabled:** Creators can prevent users from downloading their videos, making the "Save Video" button unavailable.

Using a web-based tool bypasses all of these issues, giving you a clean, high-quality copy of the original video.

First, you need the link to the video.

- In the menu that appears, tap **Copy Link**.

### Step 3: Save the Video to Your Camera Roll

This final step is where many people get stuck, but it's simple once you know where to look.
1.  After the tool processes the video, tap the **Download HD Video** button.
2.  A confirmation pop-up will appear. Tap **Download**.
