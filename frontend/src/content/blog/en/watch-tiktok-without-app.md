## Why Does TikTok Force You to Use the App?

This aggressive redirect is not a bug; it's a deliberate strategy. By pushing users into their app, TikTok can:
1.  **Increase User Numbers:** More app installs and accounts are key metrics for growth.
2.  **Gather More Data:** The app has greater access to track user behavior, including what you watch and even how you type, which is not possible in a standard web browser.

By forcing you into their ecosystem, they create a more controlled and monetizable environment. Fortunately, there are several effective ways to bypass this.

## Method 1: Use the Official TikTok Website on Desktop

The simplest way to browse TikTok without an account is on a desktop or laptop computer. The website `TikTok.com` allows you to watch videos, browse user profiles, view comments, and search for content without forcing you to log in.

The experience is less personalized, but it's perfect for casual viewing without committing to the app.

## Method 2: Use "Request Desktop Site" on Mobile Browsers

You can replicate the desktop experience on your phone. TikTok's aggressive app-redirect script specifically targets mobile browsers. By making the website think you're on a desktop, you can often bypass the block.

**How to do it on iPhone (Safari):**
1.  Open the TikTok link in Safari.
2.  Tap the **"aA"** icon in the address bar (bottom-left).
3.  Select **"Request Desktop Website"** from the menu. The page will reload, allowing you to watch the video.

**How to do it on Android (Chrome):**
1.  Open the TikTok link in Chrome.
2.  Tap the **three-dot menu** icon (usually top-right).
3.  Check the box for **"Desktop site."** The page will reload in desktop mode.

The only downside is that the layout will be small, requiring you to pinch and zoom to navigate.
