## Method 1: The Official TikTok Data Request (Safest First Step)

The most secure way to start your backup is by using TikTok's built-in data request feature. This gives you a complete record of your account activity, including a list of all your video URLs.

**How to Request Your Data:**
1.  Open the TikTok app and go to your **Profile**.
2.  Tap the **three lines** in the top-right corner to open **"Settings and privacy"**.
3.  Go to **"Account"**.
4.  Select **"Download your data"**.
5.  Choose a file format (**TXT** is human-readable, **JSON** is for developers).
6.  Tap **"Request data"** at the bottom.

It can take **24-72 hours** for TikTok to prepare your file. You'll receive a notification when it's ready to download. This file is your ultimate safety net, containing every comment, chat history, and video link associated with your account.

**Limitation:** This data file usually contains *links* to your videos, which may still have a watermark. The next method solves this.

Once you've downloaded your videos, proper organization is key.

1.  **Create a Naming System:** Rename files with the date first to keep them in chronological order (e.g., `2026-10-28_my-viral-video.mp4`).
2.  **Use Cloud Storage:** Immediately back up your video folder to a cloud service like **Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive**. A local hard drive can fail.
