Have you ever tried to long-press a GIF on Twitter (now X) to save it, only to find... nothing happens?

It's not a bug. **Twitter doesn't actually use real GIFs.** To save bandwidth, Twitter converts all uploaded GIFs into small video files (MP4s) that confirm to behave *like* GIFs.

This is why you can't just "Save Image As."

**Here is the workaround:** Use our free [Twitter Downloader](/twitter-downloader) to save the file as a video, which you can then share anywhere.

## The Easy Way (Works on Mobile & Desktop)

Since "GIFs" on Twitter are actually videos, you just need a video downloader.

### Step 1: Copy the Tweet Link
Tap the **Share icon** on the tweet containing the GIF and select **Copy Link**.

### Step 2: Paste in MediaTools
Go to [MediaTools Twitter Downloader](/twitter-downloader) and paste the link.

### Step 3: Download
Click **Get Video**. You will see the "GIF" available to download.

- **Format:** It will likely download as an **MP4 video**.
- **Why MP4?** MP4s are much smaller and higher quality than `.gif` files. All modern social apps (Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp, Discord) prefer MP4s anyway.

## How to Convert It Back to a Real "GIF"

If you absolutely need a `.gif` file formatting (e.g., for a website or email):

1. Download the MP4 using the method above.
2. Use a free tool like [Ezgif](https://ezgif.com/video-to-gif) or CloudConvert to swap it back to GIF.
3. **Warning:** The file size will likely triple, and quality may drop.

## Why Can't I Just Save It?

Twitter uses a technology called **HLS (HTTP Live Streaming)** for videos and GIFs. This splits the file into tiny chunks that load as you scroll.
